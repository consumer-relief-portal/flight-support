## Passenger Rights

### Flight Delays and Cancellations
Airlines must:
- Provide meal and refreshment allowances
- Offer hotel accommodations when appropriate
- Provide communication services
- Offer rebooking on alternative flights
- Issue refunds for cancelled flights

### Overbooking
When flights are oversold:
- Airlines must ask for volunteers first
- Compensation is required if involuntary denied boarding
- You have the right to choose cash or travel credit
- Alternative flight arrangements are required

### Baggage
- Airlines are liable for lost baggage
- Damage claims must be filed promptly
- Compensation limits apply
- Documentation speeds resolution

### Accessible Travel
- Service animals are permitted
- Wheelchair and mobility assistance available
- Special needs requests can be accommodated
- Advance notice improves service

## Filing a Complaint

**Steps to file an effective complaint:**
1. Gather documentation (boarding pass, receipts, confirmation numbers)
2. Document the issue (dates, times, locations, impacts)
3. Take photos/videos if relevant
4. Contact the airline first with written complaint
5. Keep copies of all correspondence
6. File with regulatory agencies if needed

### Regulatory Agencies

**In the United States:**
- **DOT** - Department of Transportation (federal complaints)
- **FAA** - Federal Aviation Administration (safety issues)
- **TSA** - Transportation Security Administration (security issues)

**International:**
- National aviation authorities
- Consumer protection agencies
- International aviation organizations

## Avoiding Travel Scams

**Red flags to watch for:**
- Unsolicited refund offers
- Requests for payment upfront
- Guaranteed compensation claims
- Fake airline websites
- Phishing emails posing as airlines
- Unrealistic travel deals
